# How Synthetic *Leishmania* Are Simulated

This document explains how each part of a *Leishmania* promastigote — and the
phase-contrast image it appears in — is generated by
[`synthetic_leishmania.py`](synthetic_leishmania.py). It is meant as a map of
the model: what each component represents biologically, how it is computed, and
which parameters control it.

The pipeline, end to end:

```
body geometry ─┐
               ├─► phase map ─► PhC optics ─► × amplitude texture ─► camera noise ─► image
flagellum ─────┘                  ▲
                                  └─ composited on a synthetic background
```

A single cell at a single instant is a [`ParasiteParams`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L46)
dataclass; the imaging is described by [`OpticsParams`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L22)
and [`CameraNoiseParams`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L37).

---

## 0. Units and coordinates

All **spatial** fields on `ParasiteParams` (lengths, widths, wavelengths,
amplitudes, swim speeds) and the `*_um` sigmas on `OpticsParams` are in
**micrometres**. They are converted to output **pixels** at render time by
multiplying by [`pixels_per_um(optics)`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L280)
(`1 / pixel_size_um`). The conversion happens in
[`_parasite_lengths_to_px`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L285), so the *same* cell
spec renders correctly at any magnification.

The two exceptions are `center_x` / `center_y`, which are already in **image
pixels**, and per-second quantities (frequencies, angular velocities), which
carry no length factor.

The body-axis intrinsic coordinate `s_norm`/`along` runs **−1 (posterior /
"Head" tip) → 0 (center) → +1 (anterior / flagellar tip)**. Organelles and
texture are placed in this pose-invariant frame so they stay locked to the same
material point as the cell swims and rotates.

## 1. The cell body

### 1.1 Silhouette — `body_polygon` ([L322](synthetic_leishmania.py#L322))

The outline is an **asymmetric tear-drop**: two half-ellipse profiles stitched
at the widest point.

- `body_length`, `body_width` — overall size (µm).
- `body_peak_position` ∈ [−1, 1] — where the body is widest along its axis. The
  posterior half (rounded) and anterior half (tapering to the flagellar tip)
  use different radii, giving the characteristic teardrop.
- `body_curvature` — a parabolic centerline offset `∝ (1 − s²)`, i.e. the
  "banana bend" of the cell.
- `tip_sharpness` — raises the profile to a power that grows toward both ends
  (`|s| → 1`), pinching the shoulders so the tips taper to a finer point while
  the middle is unchanged. `0` = legacy rounded (semicircular) caps.
- `body_edge_irregularity` — perturbs the upper and lower edges with
  **independent** seeded low-frequency noise (5 sinusoids each), so the
  silhouette is lumpy and asymmetric like a real high-mag body. A `(1 − s²)`
  taper anchors both tips so irregularity never detaches or balloons a tip.

Finally the polygon is rotated by `body_orientation` and translated to
`(center_x, center_y)`. Because the body **mask** is rasterised from this same
polygon, segmentation labels follow the lumps exactly.

### 1.2 Optical thickness — a 3-D model ([L823](synthetic_leishmania.py#L823))

Phase contrast responds to **optical path length** (≈ thickness), not to a flat
silhouette. The body is treated as a solid of revolution around its long axis:

1. Rasterise the polygon to a binary mask.
2. Take the interior **distance transform** `d` (distance to the nearest edge).
3. Convert to a normalised thickness `√(2·dₙ − dₙ²)`, which peaks along the
   centerline and falls to zero at the rim — i.e. the cross-section is a circle,
   so the cell looks like a rounded rod, not a flat cut-out.

### 1.3 Density envelope and organelles ([L830](synthetic_leishmania.py#L830))

Real bodies are not uniformly dark. A multiplicative `density_env` along the
body axis modulates the thickness:

- **Base envelope** — a broad Gaussian denser near the widest point
  (`0.55 + 0.45·exp(...)`).
- **Nucleus** (`nucleus_position/strength/width`) — a broad dense region, ~central.
- **Kinetoplast** (`kinetoplast_position/strength/width`) — a small, intense
  spot toward the flagellar pocket (the mitochondrial DNA disk; reads as a
  strong dark dot).
- **Cytoplasm mottle** (`cytoplasm_mottle_strength/scale`) — low-frequency
  patchiness from 4 random sinusoids along the axis (+ a mild cross-axis term),
  seeded by `body_texture_seed`.
- **Cytoplasm grain** (`cytoplasm_grain_strength/scale`) — the same
  intrinsic-coordinate sinusoid model but with more components at higher spatial
  frequency: the fine speckly intracellular texture visible at high mag.

All organelle/texture positions use the **pose-invariant** along/across axis, so
they don't flicker or wander as the cell moves.

### 1.4 Body phase ([L881](synthetic_leishmania.py#L881))

```
body_phase = thickness × body_phase_shift × density_env
```

then Gaussian-blurred by `body_edge_smooth_sigma_um` to soften the edge.
`body_phase_shift` is the overall optical density / "opacity" of the cell
(≈ 1.2 = faint, partly transparent metacyclic; ≈ 7.0 = very dark mature cell).

### 1.5 High-magnification micro-texture ([L885](synthetic_leishmania.py#L885))

At ~60–100× three discrete inclusion types appear. Crucially, they are modelled
by **two different physical mechanisms** so they read correctly through the
phase optics:

| Feature | Param prefix | Physics | How it's modelled | Appearance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Vacuoles / clear spots** | `vacuole_` | lower refractive index → **negative phase** | subtracted from `body_phase`, clipped at 0 | bright "hole" with a thin dark rim (halo/shade-off give the rim for free) |
| **Dark granules** | `granule_` | absorbing (lipid droplets, glycosomes) → **amplitude** | multiplicative darkening of a `transmission` map `(1 − depth·thickness)` | crisp black dots regardless of body phase |
| **White dots** | `whitedot_` | tiny refractile specks → **amplitude** | multiplicative brightening `(1 + boost·thickness)` | crisp white specks |

Granules are deliberately **not** phase objects: the dense body already sits past
the shade-off crossover, where *adding* phase makes a pixel brighter — so a phase
granule would come out white. Modelling them as absorption (applied to the image
*after* the optics) guarantees they stay dark.

Spots are placed in body-intrinsic coordinates via
[`_spot_field`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L926) (Poisson count × seeded Gaussian
blobs), `thickness` fading them toward the rim. The amplitude texture is returned
as the `transmission` tile and applied in `render_scene` **after** the phase
optics.

## 2. The flagellum

The flagellum is the hardest part to get right and is the model's main reference
to the literature (Wang/Wheeler **2020**, *J Cell Sci* 133:jcs246637).

### 2.1 Tangent-angle representation — `_theta_for_mode` ([L382](synthetic_leishmania.py#L382))

Instead of a `y(x)` curve, the flagellum is described by its **tangent angle**
`θ(s, t)` as a function of arclength `s_norm ∈ [0,1]` (base → tip). The centerline
is then recovered by integrating the unit tangent (§2.4). This keeps the
flagellum **inextensible** (constant length) regardless of how it bends.

The waveform follows a **static + dynamic** decomposition:

```
θ(s,t) = θ_static(s)  +  θ_dynamic(s,t)
```

### 2.2 The three beat modes (`beat_mode`)

**`tip_to_base`** — forward-swim beat ([L441](synthetic_leishmania.py#L441)).
A travelling wave `sin(k(s−L) + ωt)` with a `sin(π·s_norm)^exponent` envelope.
The sin envelope keeps the **tip roughly fixed** in tangent angle while the
middle sweeps — which is what *Leishmania* videos actually show (the tip
"points" the same direction through the beat). `tip_to_base_envelope_exponent`
reshapes that central peak.

**`base_to_tip`** — reverse / turning beat ([L457](synthetic_leishmania.py#L457)),
the more elaborate model. Built from:
- `base_to_tip_static_curl` (`sc`) — the **mean** tip tangent angle (units of π),
  i.e. the resting curl of the recovery stroke. Calibrated to Wheeler Fig 2H
  (~115°).
- `base_to_tip_static_curl_shape` (`cs`) — power law `s_norm^cs` that pushes the
  bend distally (1 = circular arc, 2 = cane/hook silhouette).
- `base_to_tip_pulse_sharpness` (`sh`) — sharpens the oscillation peaks
  (1 = pure sine).
- `base_to_tip_distal_concentration` (`dc`) — `s_norm^dc` rise envelope of the
  dynamic wave from the base.
- `base_to_tip_temporal_asymmetry` (`ta`) — anharmonic time
  `phase_t = ωt + ta·sin(ωt)` to break time-reversal (fast power stroke, slow
  recovery). Defaults to 0 (the paper's kymograph doesn't constrain it).
- `base_to_tip_propagation_extent` (`pe`) — a `tanh` falloff setting how far
  along the flagellum the wave keeps amplitude. `1.0` = reaches the tip (visible
  power-stroke tip sweep); `<1` = the partially-propagating phenotype.

**`static`** — paralysed / immotile ([L416](synthetic_leishmania.py#L416)).
`static_mode_curl = 0` → straight (dead / recently divided). Non-zero →
the curled-paralysed phenotype of axoneme mutants (dIC140, dHydin; Wheeler 2020
Fig 6), curling with the **opposite** polarity (negative sign) into a >360°
spiral.

Each mode reads its own frequency / wavelength / amplitude (`tip_to_base_*`,
`base_to_tip_*`), falling back via [`_coalesce`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L264)
to the generic `beat_*` values when the per-mode override is `None`.

### 2.3 Mode switching and blending ([L496](synthetic_leishmania.py#L496))

*Leishmania* stochastically switch between forward (tip-to-base) and turning
(base-to-tip) beats. `mode_switch_rate` drives a Poisson schedule
([`generate_mode_schedule`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L546)), or an explicit
`mode_schedule` can be supplied. Around each switch,
[`_get_active_mode_blend`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L496) returns two modes and a
raised-cosine `blend` over `mode_transition_duration`, and
[`beat_tangent_angle`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L533) linearly interpolates the
two tangent-angle profiles so the beat morphs smoothly rather than snapping.

### 2.4 Centerline reconstruction — `flagellum_curve` ([L565](synthetic_leishmania.py#L565))

Given `θ(s)`, the curve is built by stepping along the unit tangent and
cumulatively summing: `x = Σ cos θ·ds`, `y = Σ sin θ·ds`. The result is rotated
into the body frame and **attached to the anterior pole** (`+body_length/2`
along the body axis), so the flagellum emerges from the front of the cell.

### 2.5 Second flagellum (dividing cells) — `second_flagellum_curve` ([L586](synthetic_leishmania.py#L586))

A dividing promastigote grows a second flagellum from a new basal body in a new
pocket immediately adjacent to the old one. When `second_flagellum_enabled`:

- length = `flagellum_length × second_flagellum_length_scale`,
- starts at the **same anterior pole**, offset by
  `second_flagellum_lateral_offset` (the ~0.3–0.6 µm pocket spacing, unresolvable
  in PhC) and tilted by `second_flagellum_angle_offset` (a few degrees of
  divergence — the new flagellum is untethered in *Leishmania*),
- shares the primary waveform but with `second_flagellum_phase_offset` added, so
  the two beat slightly out of sync.

Keypoints follow the **primary** flagellum's chain but the second is emitted as
its own `Flag2_*` / `Tip2` chain (see §4).

### 2.6 Rasterisation ([L985](synthetic_leishmania.py#L985))

The centerline is drawn as an antialiased thick polyline (`cv2.polylines`,
`LINE_AA`) at its **true physical width** (`flagellum_width`) with phase value
`flagellum_phase_shift`, into its own buffer, then **max-composited** onto the
body phase. The second flagellum is drawn into the same buffer.

> **Label vs. image split:** a real ~0.25 µm flagellum is ≤ 1 px at low mag — too
> thin to be a usable segmentation target. The *image* keeps the physically-thin
> line, but the *mask* width is floored at
> [`MIN_FLAGELLUM_MASK_PX = 3`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L692) (with
> [`_polyline_thickness_for_width`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L695) correcting cv2's
> line-fattening so the rasterised width is exact). This widens only the label.

## 3. Beat-driven body wobble — `_apply_body_wobble` ([L630](synthetic_leishmania.py#L630))

At low Reynolds number the cell body can't absorb the flagellar beat: each
stroke's lateral drag reaction shows up as a small body wiggle + yaw at the beat
frequency. Modelled as a pure sinusoid at the active mode's frequency:

- `body_lateral_wobble_amplitude` — lateral translation (µm, ⊥ to body axis),
- `body_yaw_wobble_amplitude` — body rotation (rad),
- `body_wobble_phase_lag` — offset from the beat phase.

This is applied **at render time only** — the underlying swim state
(`center`, `body_orientation`) is untouched in `advance_parasites`, so the wobble
oscillates without drifting the cell. Body, both flagella, and keypoints all
derive from the wobbled pose, so they move together.

## 4. Keypoints (the SLEAP skeleton) — `_make_keypoints` ([L717](synthetic_leishmania.py#L717))

A fixed skeleton is emitted per cell:

```
Head  →  Base  →  Flag1 … FlagN  →  Tip          (N = n_flagellum_keypoints, default 6)
```

- **Head** — posterior body tip (`center − body_length/2` along the axis).
- **Base** — the flagellum's first point (anterior pole).
- **Flag1…N** — evenly spaced interior samples along the flagellum
  ([`_flag_chain_points`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L710)).
- **Tip** — flagellum endpoint.

Dividing cells additionally emit `Flag2_1…N` and `Tip2` for the second
flagellum, so a single two-flagellum skeleton applies to every cell — ordinary
cells simply leave those nodes unlabeled (NaN / not-visible in SLEAP).

Occlusion-aware visibility is resolved later in `render_scene` (§9).

---

## 5. Dividing cells — `make_dividing_pair` ([L1049](synthetic_leishmania.py#L1049))

A pre-cytokinesis cell is modelled as **one** `ParasiteParams` (not two), since
the "one body, two flagella" stage dominates the dividing-looking phenotype in
PhC. Given a `division_stage ∈ [0,1]` it adjusts a template:

- **widens** the body (`width_boost = 1.4 + 0.6·stage`) → aspect drops ~8:1 → ~4:1,
- pushes `body_peak_position` toward center → oval/almond, not teardrop,
- adds a gentle **sigmoid bend** as the basal-body units segregate,
- places **two dark organelle patches** (duplicated nucleus + kinetoplast that
  drift apart with stage),
- enables the **second flagellum**, growing from 0.25 → 0.70 of the primary
  length, with stage-dependent lateral offset / divergence and a random phase.

## 6. Phase-contrast optics — `simulate_phase_contrast` ([L1174](synthetic_leishmania.py#L1174))

Converts the accumulated phase map `φ` into intensity with a hybrid model tuned
to look like real positive (Ph1) phase contrast:

1. **PSF blur** — Gaussian blur of `φ` by `psf_sigma_um`.
2. **Exp darkening** — `exp(−intensity_gain·φ)`: dense regions go dark grey,
   background stays ~1.
3. **Halo** — a difference-of-Gaussians, `halo_strength·max(outer − inner, 0)`,
   producing the bright halo that rings phase objects.
4. **Shade-off** — a numerically-stable softplus around `shadeoff_threshold`,
   the brightening of the *interior* of large dense objects (and the mechanism
   that makes the granule-vs-phase distinction in §1.5 necessary).

[`simulate_phase_contrast_fast`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L1222) is a
visually-equivalent variant that computes the large halo blur at downsampled
resolution and reuses `φ` as the inner term.

After the optics, the image is multiplied by the amplitude `transmission` map
(granules/white dots, §1.5) and the background (§8).

## 7. Camera noise — `add_camera_noise` ([L1208](synthetic_leishmania.py#L1208))

A realistic sCMOS model (`CameraNoiseParams`):

- **Poisson shot noise** — scale to photons by `full_well_photons`, sample
  `Poisson`, scale back.
- **Gaussian read noise** — `read_noise_e / full_well_photons`.
- **Dark offset** — `dark_offset`.

[`add_camera_noise_fast`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L1267) replaces the per-pixel
Poisson draw with its Gaussian approximation `N(N, √N)`, exact to 3+ decimals at
realistic photon counts.

---

## 8. Synthetic background and clutter — `synthetic_background` ([L1291](synthetic_leishmania.py#L1291))

The cells are composited onto a procedurally generated PhC background whose job
is to teach the model what is **not** a cell. `clutter_level` scales all artifact
counts linearly (intensities sub-linearly). Components:

- vignetting + slow illumination tilt,
- multi-scale texture (low-freq blotches, mid grime, fine grain),
- **dust** particles and high-density **fine specks**,
- out-of-focus **debris rings** (Newton/Airy), circular **ghost cells**, and
  **elongated ring** artifacts — the classic false-positive trap that looks
  vaguely cell-shaped,
- **hairs / filaments** — long curving dark lines with a subtle halo (must not be
  mistaken for a flagellum),
- **scratches**, **bubbles** (bright interior, dark rim — opposite of dust),
  and **crystals** (sharp angular edges).

---

## 9. Scene compositing and occlusion — `render_scene` ([L1739](synthetic_leishmania.py#L1739))

Renders every parasite into a local bbox tile and combines them:

1. **Phase compositing** — tiles are **max-composited** into a full-frame phase
   map. A `winner_map` records, per pixel, which cell index has the largest phase
   contribution (used for both occlusion and masks).
2. **Occlusion-aware keypoints** — for each keypoint, a two-step rule:
   *(a)* if the cell **wins at the exact keypoint pixel**, it's visible (protects
   thin-flagellum keypoints next to a big neighbour); *(b)* otherwise a patch
   vote — visible only if the cell owns ≥ `occlusion_majority_threshold` of the
   patch's non-background pixels. Occluded keypoints are dropped (→ NaN in SLEAP).
3. **Instance masks** ([`_resolve_instance_masks`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L1665))
   — per-cell body / flagellum masks resolved against the winner map, providing
   both **amodal** (`*_full`, true extent ignoring occlusion) and **modal**
   (visible-only) masks, a `visible_frac`, and per-flagellum masks so each
   flagellum can be a separate YOLO instance under one `animal_id`.
4. **Final image** — `background × intensity × transmission`, then camera noise,
   clipped to [0, 1].

## 10. Motion / advancing in time — `advance_parasites` ([L1994](synthetic_leishmania.py#L1994))

Between frames, each cell's pose is integrated by `dt`:

- `tip_to_base` → **forward swim** (`swim_speed` along the body axis).
- `base_to_tip` → **turn in place**: an impulse-and-decay rotation
  ([`get_active_motion`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L1961) /
  [`_motion_pulse_factor`](synthetic_leishmania.py#L1917)) that bursts during the
  power stroke and decays during recovery, so turning is pulsed rather than
  continuous. The base-to-tip turn has a fixed sign (the beat's fixed
  polarisation); lab-frame diversity comes from random `body_orientation`.
- `static` → no motion.

Mode blending applies here too, and `swim_speed` is converted µm/s → px/s via
the optics when provided.

---

## 11. Random sampling — `sample_random_parasite` ([L2020](synthetic_leishmania.py#L2020))

Draws one realistic cell covering the phenotypic spread the model should learn:

- body 8–18 µm × 1.2–3.5 µm (procyclic → metacyclic), flagellum 5–30 µm,
- per-mode beat parameters (tip-to-base ~15–28 Hz, base-to-tip ~2–10 Hz) and the
  full set of beat-shape knobs from §2,
- ~5% **paralysed** cells (split ~40% straight / ~60% spiral-curled),
- ~50% of motile cells get stochastic mode switching,
- `body_phase_shift` 1.2–7.0 for a wide opacity range,
- small beat-driven wobble.

`render_scene` is the entry point that turns a list of these into a labelled
phase-contrast frame.

## Quick parameter index

| Cell part | Key parameters |
|---|---|
| Body shape | `body_length`, `body_width`, `body_peak_position`, `body_curvature`, `tip_sharpness`, `body_edge_irregularity` |
| Body opacity / texture | `body_phase_shift`, `nucleus_*`, `kinetoplast_*`, `cytoplasm_mottle_*`, `cytoplasm_grain_*`, `body_texture_seed` |
| Micro-texture | `granule_*`, `vacuole_*`, `whitedot_*`, `microtexture_seed` |
| Flagellum geometry | `flagellum_length`, `flagellum_width`, `flagellum_phase_shift`, `n_flagellum_keypoints` |
| Beat | `beat_mode`, `beat_frequency`, `beat_wavelength`, `beat_amplitude_max`, per-mode `tip_to_base_*` / `base_to_tip_*`, `static_mode_curl` |
| Mode switching | `mode_switch_rate`, `mode_schedule`, `mode_transition_duration` |
| Second flagellum | `second_flagellum_enabled`, `second_flagellum_length_scale`, `second_flagellum_lateral_offset`, `second_flagellum_angle_offset`, `second_flagellum_phase_offset` |
| Body wobble | `body_lateral_wobble_amplitude`, `body_yaw_wobble_amplitude`, `body_wobble_phase_lag` |
| Motion | `swim_speed`, `angular_velocity`, per-mode overrides, `rotation_rise/decay_tau_cycles` |
| Optics | `pixel_size_um`, `psf_sigma_um`, `halo_strength`, `halo_lowpass_sigma_um`, `intensity_gain`, `shadeoff_threshold/strength`, `body_edge_smooth_sigma_um` |
| Camera | `full_well_photons`, `read_noise_e`, `dark_offset`, `bg_intensity` |

*Primary biological reference throughout the flagellum model: Wang, Wheeler &
Carrington (2020), *J Cell Sci* 133:jcs246637, plus Wheeler/Gluenz/Gull
(2011, 2013) for the dividing-cell phenotype.*
